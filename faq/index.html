How We Work

The SGAI Code of Conduct outlines operational standards and expected behavior throughout all SGAI activities. SGAI values the ideals of open exchange of ideas, the freedom of thought and expression, and respectful science based debate. This requires a community and an environment that recognizes and respects the inherent dignity and worth of every person.

As such, this Code of Conduct applies to all SGAI activities, including but not limited to:

  • All internal activities.

  • External events organized by, hosted by, or run in collaboration with SGAI.

  • All communications, media items, and other public outreach published by or through SGAI channels, such as the SGAI website or social media.

All SGAI affiliates (volunteers, employees, contractors, etc.) as well as participants, speakers, mentors, mentees, panelists, area chairs, reviewers, sponsors, contractors, organizers, and participants at SGAI-affiliated activities as suggested above are expected to comply with this Code of Conduct.





What we do.

Responsibilities & Expected Behavior


All the members of SGAI, as outlined above, should help cultivate an environment for productive, creative exchange by following these guidelines. In particular, our community is built on the principles of intellectual diversity and humility, constructive discussion, and scientific integrity. This also includes a set of ground rules for interactions between community members interacting on SGAI platforms or in the context of SGAI organized or affiliated events:

  • The SGAI community encourages an overall warm and welcoming atmosphere. Treat your fellow community members politely and respectfully.

  • Respect the diverse background of SGAI community members. Remember that others might not share your jargon, may be unfamiliar with what you think is common knowledge, and may not speak English (or another language being used for communication) as a first language.

  • We value honesty and respect for the ownership of ideas. Do not post materials that belong to someone else without their permission, do not publicly share private individuals’ information, and do not violate intellectual property laws. It is important that we are able to trust one another – do not misrepresent your or others’ work, and be sure to properly attribute ideas if sharing them elsewhere.


Unacceptable Behavior


SGAI is dedicated to fostering an environment free from harassment, bullying, discrimination, and retaliation. This includes offensive comments related to sex, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, nationality, race, ethnicity, religion (or lack thereof), age, disability, physical appearance, body size, technology choices, education level, socioeconomic status, or any other personal characteristics, as well as further actions or considerations made unlawful by national or local laws, ordinances, or regulations.

Inappropriate or unprofessional behavior will not be tolerated in either physical or virtual settings. This includes bullying, intimidation, personal attacks, harassment, sustained disruption of talks or other events, sexual harassment, stalking, following, harassing photography or recording, inappropriate physical contact, unwelcome sexual attention, public vulgar exchanges, derogatory name-calling, or diminutive characterizations, all of which are unwelcome in this community. Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviour, is also considered harassment.

No use of images, activities, or other materials that are of a sexual, racial, or otherwise offensive nature that may create an inappropriate or toxic environment is permitted. Disorderly, boisterous, or disruptive conduct including but not limited to fighting, coercion, theft, damage to property, or any mistreatment towards other individuals is not tolerated. Scientific misconduct—including but not limited to fabrication, falsification, or plagiarism of research or ideas in written or spoken form—is prohibited.

Who are we?


We are an AI inspired Climate Change Mitigation and Adaptation Company that uses the principles of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning to highlight and document the following unique areas;

  1. Predictive modeling: With AI we can help predict the future impact of climate change and provide early warning systems for natural disasters such as hurricanes, heatwaves, Earthquakes and droughts.

  2. Energy Efficiency: With AI we intend to optimize energy consumption in buildings, factories, and transportation systems, reducing carbon emissions and helping to mitigate the effects of climate change.

  3. Renewable Energy: With AI we intend to improve the efficiency of renewable energy sources such as wind and solar power, allowing for more widespread adoption of these technologies.

  4. Sustainable Agriculture: With AI we intend to optimize farming practices and improve food production, reducing greenhouse gas emissions from agriculture and helping to create more sustainable food systems.

  5. Conservation and Biodiversity: With AI we intend to monitor and protect wildlife and their habitats, helping to conserve biodiversity and reduce the negative impacts of climate change on wildlife populations.

Where and How are we going to use ML and AI in the fight Against Climate Change?


We recognize that Artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) have the potential to play a significant role in addressing the challenge of climate change. Here are some examples of areas where AI and ML could be applied:

  1. Energy: AI and ML algorithms can help optimize energy production and consumption, making it more efficient and reducing waste. For example, machine learning algorithms can help predict energy demand and adjust energy production in real-time.

  2. Agriculture: AI and ML can help improve agricultural practices, making them more sustainable and reducing their carbon footprint. For example, algorithms can analyze data from sensors on crops and soil to optimize irrigation and fertilization, reducing water usage and fertilizer runoff.

  3. Climate modeling: Climate models are essential for understanding and predicting the impacts of climate change. AI and ML can help improve the accuracy of these models by analyzing large amounts of data from various sources and helping identify patterns and relationships.

  4. Renewable energy: AI and ML can help optimize the deployment and management of renewable energy sources, such as wind and solar, improving their efficiency and reducing their costs.

  5. Carbon capture: AI and ML can help optimize carbon capture and storage technologies, which are essential for mitigating the impacts of greenhouse gas emissions.

  6. Environmental monitoring: AI and ML can help monitor the environment and detect changes, such as deforestation and species migration, in near real-time, allowing for more effective conservation efforts.

These are just a few examples of how AI and ML can be applied in the fight against climate change. However, it is important to note that AI and ML alone will not solve the problem of climate change, and that a multi-disciplinary approach is required, including policy changes, public education, and investment in clean energy technologies.

How are we in SGAI using AI and ML to work in this Climate Change Resilience, Mitigation and Adaptation?


Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) can play a crucial role in addressing the challenges posed by climate change by providing innovative solutions in the areas of resilience, mitigation, and adaptation. Some of the specific applications are:

  1. Resilience: AI and ML can be used to create early warning systems for natural disasters such as hurricanes, droughts, and floods. These systems can use predictive analytics to help communities better prepare for and respond to these events.

  2. Mitigation: AI and ML can be used to improve energy efficiency, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and optimize energy production from renewable sources. For example, AI algorithms can be used to monitor energy usage in buildings and homes and identify ways to reduce waste and optimize energy usage.

  3. Adaptation: AI and ML can be used to improve land-use planning, water management, and agriculture. For example, AI algorithms can be used to monitor and analyze soil moisture levels, crop growth, and weather patterns to help farmers make more informed decisions about planting and harvesting crops.

  4. Climate modeling: AI and ML can also be used to improve climate models and provide more accurate predictions of future climate conditions. This information can help policymakers and communities plan for the future and make decisions about how to adapt to a changing climate.

Overall, AI and ML offer tremendous potential to help address the challenges posed by climate change and support more sustainable and resilient communities.
